Background

Denim is a strong, durable cotton warp-faced textile used in making jeans and other type of clothes. It is thought to have its origin at Nimes town in France.

The appearance of denim since the mid-19th century is a game changer for the fashion industry today. The demand for more durable and quality clothing is increasingly becoming a priority. Denim popularity all over the world has earn jeans wear a prominent stage in the world market.

Young and old people passion for denim clothing is on the rise. The availability of jeans in different colors and designs gives young people a variety of denim fabrics and garments to choose from. The responsive pictures below are some examples of denim fabrics:

The following are some of the different existing forms of denim fabrics extensively used in the fashion industry:

  • Stretch denim
  • Pseudo denim
  • Generic denim
  • Reverse denim

The combination of cotton with different fibres has improved the functionality of the different denim materials one would love to have in their wardrobe.

The existence of denim for over a century is indicative of the importance of this fabric in the fashion industry and to the society in general.
