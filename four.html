Environmental Impact of Denim Production

The environmental impact of denim is significant in the manufacturing steps such as cotton growing, dyeing, and washing.

The various steps of denim processing are associated with the following problems:

  1. Water consumption
    • The finishing processes and wear of a single pair of jeans take about 2900 gallons of water.
  2. Water pollution
    • Heavy usage of herbicides and pesticides in cotton growing contaminates the soil and water sources.
    • Additionally, the health of workers is at risk due to exposure to harmful chemical sprays.
  3. Denim sandblasting
    • Silica, the main ingredient used in denim abrasive blasting, is harmful to the workers.

The following are some possible solutions to the potential problems associated with denim manufacturing:

  • Use of organic cotton
  • Reduction of dyes and chemicals
  • less complex wastewater production
